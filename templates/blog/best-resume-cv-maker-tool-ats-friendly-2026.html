What ATS-friendly really means

  • Clear section headings like Summary, Skills, Experience, and Education.
  • Simple layout with readable text.
  • No confusing tables or design blocks that break parsing.
  • Keywords used naturally, not stuffed.

What recruiters want to see

Recruiters still care about impact. A builder that helps you write measurable bullets is more valuable than one that only changes colors. A recruiter-friendly CV shows role relevance, outcome, and clarity in the first few seconds.

For example, instead of writing "responsible for social media", you can write "grew Instagram engagement by 22% through a 6-week content calendar." That sounds more credible and helps the resume read like a real work story.

What to avoid

  • Too much design and not enough text structure.
